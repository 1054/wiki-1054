==== Star Formation Rate ====

Star formation rate (SFR) is the rate at which new stars are being formed, in unit of solar mass per year. In low redshift star-forming galaxies, dense molecular gas condense and collapse, then groups of dense cores/filaments/sheets appear. Larger/more massive dense cores evolve faster to the hydrogen fusion step, and step from their pre-main-sequence phase into their main-sequence phase. During these processes (gas collapsing, gas accretion by the stars, and gas and dust heating by the emitted photons), photons are emitted through both hot (~few thousand Kelvin) gaseous black-body radiations and atomic/molecular quantum energy level transitions. Larger/more massive stars like O,B type stars have the most energetic emissions, thus are the easiest to detect in UV and optical bands and in quantum transition spectra. While for all new stars, the dust heating gives us another view of star formation in the IR band: the star-formation-induced photons heat the dust grains to certain (~few tens Kelvin) temperatures, then the black-body radiations of heated dust grains are the powerful evidences of star formation. 

In practical, UV, optical, and hydrogen quantum transitions generally probe the O,B SFRs, while IR and molecular quantum transitions generally probe the overall SFRs (but blended with some non-star-formation radiation). 


==== SFR indicators ====
  * UV Continuum
  * Opt Continuum Integration
  * Opt Recombination Lines (Lyalpha n=2->1, Lybeta n=3->1, ..., Halpha n=3->2, Hbeta n=4->2, ..., etc.)
  * IR Continuum
  * NIR Rotational Transition Lines (H2 J=X->X-1 S(0/1), etc.) 
  * FIR Rotational Transition Lines (CO J=X->X-1, CS, HCN, HNC, HCO+, etc.)
  * Radio Continuum

==== SFR calibrations ====
  * 1.4 GHz Continuum
    * $\displaystyle SFR_{(M \ge 5 M_{sun})} \, = \frac{L_{1.4GHz}}{4.0\times10^{21} W Hz^{-1}} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1}$ ($\Phi_{M} \propto M^{-2.5}$ IMF) (Condon 1992) ([[http://www.physics.usyd.edu.au/~ahopkins/thesis/node11.html | Hopkins]])
  * 6563 A H-alpha
    * $\displaystyle SFR_{(M \ge 5 M_{sun})} \, = \frac{L_{H\alpha}}{1.5\times10^{34} W} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1}$ ($\Phi_{M} \propto M^{-2.5}$ IMF) (Kennicutt 1983) ([[http://www.physics.usyd.edu.au/~ahopkins/thesis/node11.html | Hopkins]])
  * 60 um Continuum
    * $\displaystyle SFR_{(M \ge 5 M_{sun})} \, = \frac{L_{60 \mu m}}{5.1\times10^{23} W Hz^{-1}} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1}$ ($\Phi_{M} \propto M^{-2.5}$ IMF) (Condon 1992) ([[http://www.physics.usyd.edu.au/~ahopkins/thesis/node11.html | Hopkins]])
  * 24 um Continuum
    * $\displaystyle SFR = \frac{L_{24 \mu m}}{0.49\times10^{43} erg s^{-1}} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1} \ \ (4 \times 10^{42} erg s^{-1} \le L_{24 \mu m} \le 5 \times 10^{43} erg s^{-1})$ (Kroupa IMF) (Wu et al. 2005; Zhu et al. 2008; Kennicutt et al. 2009; Rieke et al. 2009) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sept12/Calzetti/Calzetti1_2.html | Calzetti]])
    * $\displaystyle SFR = \frac{L_{24 \mu m}}{0.49\times10^{43} erg s^{-1}} \times (\frac{L_{24 \mu m}}{0.49\times10^{44} erg s^{-1}})^{0.048} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1} \ \ (L_{24 \mu m} > 5 \times 10^{43} erg s^{-1})$ (Kroupa IMF) (Wu et al. 2005; Zhu et al. 2008; Kennicutt et al. 2009; Rieke et al. 2009) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sept12/Calzetti/Calzetti1_2.html | Calzetti]])
  * 70 um Continuum
    * $\displaystyle SFR = \frac{L_{70 \mu m}}{0.17\times10^{44} erg s^{-1}} \ M_{sun} yr^{-1} \ \ (L_{70 \mu m} > 1.4 \times 10^{42} erg s^{-1})$ (Kroupa IMF) (Calzetti et al. 2010) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sept12/Calzetti/Calzetti1_2.html | Calzetti]])
  * IR
    * $\displaystyle SFR = \frac{L_{IR,8-1000\mu m}}{10^{10}\,L_{\odot}}$ (Kennicutt 1998; Genzel et al. 2010)
    * $\displaystyle SFR = \frac{1.3 \, L_{FIR,40-400\mu m}}{10^{10}\,L_{\odot}}$ (Kennicutt 1998; Carilli & Walter 2013)






==== LIR calibrations ====
  * FIR ($40-400 \mu m$) flux from IRAS
    * $\displaystyle F_{FIR(40-400 \mu m)} \, = 1.26 \times 10^{-14} \, (2.58 \, \frac{f_{60}}{Jy} + \frac{f_{100}}{Jy}) \ W m^{-2}$ (Lonsdale et al. 1985 [[https://books.google.fr/books?id=y3zvAAAAMAAJ | Book]]; Sanders et al. 2003) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sanders/Sanders2.html | Sanders]] & [[http://www.sao.ru/cats/doc/IRAS_galqso.html | IRAS_galqso]])
  * FIR ($40-500 \mu m$) luminosity from IRAS
    * $\displaystyle L_{FIR(40-500 \mu m)} \, = 4 \pi \, D_{L}^2 \, C \, \frac{F_{FIR}}{Jy} \ L_{\odot}$ (Lonsdale et al. 1985 [[https://books.google.fr/books?id=y3zvAAAAMAAJ | Book]]; Sanders et al. 2003) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sanders/Sanders2.html | Sanders]])
    * $C$ (typically in the range $1.4-1.8$) is the correction factor required to account principally for the extrapolated flux longward of the IRAS 100 Âµm filter. (Sanders et al. 2003) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sanders/Sanders2.html | Sanders]])
  * IR ($8-1000 \mu m$) flux from IRAS
    * $\displaystyle F_{IR(8-1000 \mu m)} \, = 1.8 \times 10^{-14} \, (13.48 \, \frac{f_{12}}{Jy} + 5.16 \, \frac{f_{25}}{Jy} + 2.58 \, \frac{f_{60}}{Jy} + \frac{f_{100}}{Jy}) \ W m^{-2}$ (Lonsdale et al. 1985 [[https://books.google.fr/books?id=y3zvAAAAMAAJ | Book]]; Sanders et al. 2003) ([[http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sanders/Sanders2.html | Sanders]] & [[http://www.sao.ru/cats/doc/IRAS_galqso.html | IRAS_galqso]])
